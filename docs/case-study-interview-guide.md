# Case Study Interview Guide

## Purpose
Extract compelling stories from successful JOCstudio users for marketing content.

## Interview Format
- **Duration:** 15-20 minutes
- **Method:** Zoom (recorded) or phone
- **Recording:** Yes, with permission
- **Output:** Written case study + quotes + social content

---

### Research Subject:
- Check their JOCstudio usage (projects completed, time saved)

## The Interview Questions

### Opening (2 min)
**1. Tell me about you and your company.**
- Name, role, company name
- How long in business?
- What trades do you specialize in?
- Team size?

*Goal: Establish context and humanize them*

---

### The Before (3 min)
**2. Walk me through your estimating process before JOCstudio.**
- What tools were you using?
- How long did a typical JOC proposal take?
- What was the most frustrating part?
- Tell me about a time when the old way caused a problem.

*Listen for: Specific pain points, emotional language, time wasted*

**3. What was at stake? What would happen if you didn't fix this?**
- Were you losing bids?
- Working too many hours?
- Missing family time?
- Losing money on errors?

*Listen for: Stakes, urgency, emotional impact*

---

### The Discovery (2 min)
**4. How did you find JOCstudio? What made you give it a try?**
- Google search? Referral? LinkedIn?
- What was the "aha" moment?
- What almost stopped you from signing up?

*Listen for: Marketing channel validation, objections overcome*

---

### The Transformation (5 min)
**5. Tell me about your first win with JOCstudio.**
- What was the project?
- How long did it take vs. before?
- Walk me through the moment you submitted the proposal.
- How did you feel?

*Listen for: Specific numbers, emotional peak, contrast with before*

**6. What's different now?**
- How many contracts have you won since?
- Total time saved?
- Money made/saved?
- How has your business grown?
- What can you do now that you couldn't before?

*Listen for: Concrete results, ROI, business impact*

---

### The Future (3 min)
**7. What's next for you and your company?**
- Growth plans?
- Hiring?
- Bigger contracts?

**8. What would you tell another contractor considering JOCstudio?**
- The one thing they should know?
- Who is it perfect for?
- Who is it NOT for?

*Listen for: Testimonial gold, referral language*

---

## Follow-Up Questions (Use as needed)

### If answers are vague:
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "What did that look like day-to-day?"
- "How much time exactly?"

### If answers are emotional:
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "What was going through your mind?"
- "How did that make you feel?"

### If they mention other tools:
- "Why didn't [competitor] work for you?"
- "What made you switch?"

### For better quotes:
- "If you had to describe JOCstudio in one sentence..."
- "Complete this sentence: 'JOCstudio is the only tool that...'"
- "What would you tell your past self?"

---

## Closing (2 min)

### Thank them:
"Thank you so much for sharing your story. This is exactly why we built JOCstudio."

### Set expectations:
"I'll write up the case study and send it to you for review before we publish. Should have it to you within a week."

### Ask for referrals:
"Do you know any other contractors who might have a similar story? I'd love to feature them too."

## Recording Tips

### Technical:
- Test recording before call
- Backup recording (phone recorder)
- Good lighting for video
- Quiet environment

### Interview Style:
- Listen more than talk (70/30 rule)
- Don't rush â€” silence is okay
- Follow interesting tangents
- Ask "why" and "how" often
- Get emotional responses

### Make Them Comfortable:
- Start with easy questions
- Validate their answers ("That's incredible")
- Share your own brief story
- Thank them throughout
- Be genuinely curious

---

## Red Flags (Bad Interview)

- Generic answers
- No specific numbers
- Can't remember details
- Only positive, no struggle
- Rushing through

**If this happens:**
- Ask more follow-ups
- Switch to easier questions
- Consider not publishing (quality over quantity)

---

## Green Flags (Great Interview)

- Emotional language
- Specific numbers
- Story arcs
- "Before/after" contrast
- Enthusiasm
- Referrals

**If this happens:**
- Dig deeper on best parts
- Ask for referrals immediately
- Plan multi-channel content (blog, social, video)
